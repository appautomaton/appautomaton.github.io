# Plate sources

All engravings are public domain or CC0. The site never recolors the files
themselves — CSS filters re-tone them live: sepia-ink on celadon paper by
day, limelight silver-sage on the dark stage by night.

- `stage-trio.webp` — "Jouets Mécaniques de Vaucanson ; Un Sauvage, un
  berger provencal et un canard" (cropped), the theatrical presentation of
  Vaucanson's three automata, 1738. Public domain.
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Jouets_M%C3%A9caniques_de_Vaucanson_;_Un_Sauvage,_un_berger_provencal_et_un_canard_cropped.jpg

- `duck.webp` — the digesting duck alone on its pedestal (further-cropped
  variant of the same 1738 engraving). Public domain.

- `cartouche.webp` — rocaille cartouche designed to carry a series title,
  1712–1755. CC0, Rijksmuseum (RP-P-1883-A-7511), cropped to the ornament.
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Rocaille_cartouche,_RP-P-1883-A-7511.jpg

- `cloud-machine.webp` — "Machines de Théatres, pl. XV", cloud-machine
  stage rigging, Encyclopédie, 1772. Public Domain Mark 1.0, Cooper Hewitt,
  Smithsonian Design Museum (1977-86-34).
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Print,_Machines_de_Théatres_(Theatrical_Machinery),_pl._XV,_vol._10_from_Encyclopédie,_ou,_arts_et_des_métiers_(Encyclopedia,_or_a_Systematic_Dictionary_of_the_Sciences,_Arts,_and_Crafts),_1772_(CH_18493439).jpg

- `comedie-section.webp` — the Comédie-Française in longitudinal section:
  stage, machinery loft, and auditorium, Encyclopédie vol. 10, 1772. Public
  Domain Mark 1.0.
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Diderot%27s_Encyclopédie,_Vol10_(1772),_Theatres,_Comédie_Française,_plate3_-_longitudinal_section_-_Dover_reprint_1995.jpg

- `schouwburg.webp` — a performance in the Amsterdam Schouwburg seen
  through the proscenium, 1738 (the same year as the Vaucanson engraving).
  CC0, Rijksmuseum (RP-P-OB-84.778).
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Schouwburg_met_toneeldecor_De_Berg_van_Parnas_de_Berg_van_Parnas_(titel_op_object)_Toneeldecors_van_de_Amsterdamse_Schouwburg,_ca._1760_(serietitel),_RP-P-OB-84.778.jpg

- `imprimerie.webp` — Encyclopédie "Imprimerie en Lettres", pl. 1: the
  composing room. CC0.
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Planche_de_l%E2%80%99Encyclop%C3%A9die_de_Diderot_et_d%E2%80%99Alembert._Pl._1._Imprimerie_en_Lettres,_L%E2%80%99Op%C3%A9ration_de_la_casse,_G.33153.jpg

- `camera-obscura.webp` — portable camera obscura from Athanasius
  Kircher's Ars Magna Lucis et Umbrae, 1646. Public domain.
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:1646_Athanasius_Kircher_-_Camera_obscura.jpg

- `magic-lantern.webp` — a magic lantern projecting portraits, French
  engraving, 1795. CC0.
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Un_petit_Amour_d%C3%A9couvre_gr%C3%A2ce_%C3%A0_une_lanterne_magique,_les_portraits_de_Louis_XVI,_de_Marie_Antoinette,_de_Madame_Elisabe,_G.52989.jpg

- `serinette.webp` — Laurent Cars after Chardin, "La Serinette": a lady
  playing a mechanical bird-organ. Public domain.
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Laurent_Cars_-_La_Serinette_(Lady_with_a_Bird-Organ).jpg

- `durer-perspective.webp` — Dürer's perspective apparatus drawing a lute,
  Underweysung der Messung, 1525. Public domain.
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Duerer_Underweysung_der_Messung_fig_001_page_181.jpg

Converted to WebP with cwebp from the originals, otherwise untouched.
